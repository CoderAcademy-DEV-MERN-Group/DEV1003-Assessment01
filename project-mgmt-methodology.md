## Agile Project Management Methodology

Agile is an iterative methodology that emphasises adaptability, collaboration and incremental progress (Atlassian, 2024). Agile's core principles include working in short development cycles, continuous delivery and feedback, and responding to change rather than rigid planning. Working in an Agile approach helps maintain progress while adapting to changes through practices including sprints (short work cycles), daily stand ups (quick team check-ins), and retrospectives (reviewing what worked and what to improve).

We adopted Agile as our overarching methodology because it suits a small team working on a complex project that requires flexibility and regular adjustments. Agile allows us to:

- Adapt quickly to change
- Collaborate and maintain transparency
- Deliver incremental progress in short cycles
- Continuously reflect and improve

### Agile Practices in Our Project

- Task Planning
- Prioritisation
- Pipeline Management


- **Sprints:** Weekly cycles delivering incremental outcomes aligned with MVP requirements.
- **Daily Stand-ups:** Short Slack updates covering yesterday’s work, today’s goals, and blockers.
- **Retrospectives:** End-of-sprint reviews to evaluate what worked and what needs adjustment.
- **Task Prioritisation:** Dependencies and MVP-aligned tasks addressed first.

### Example: Agile in Practice

Phase 1 – Planning, Design & Documentation (Week 1):

1. Defined sprint goals aligned with rubric MVPs.
2. Held daily Slack stand-ups. Example format:
    - Yesterday’s work + time spent
    - Today’s plan
    - Completion % toward phase goal
    - Low vs high-priority blockers
3. Closed sprint with a retrospective and adjusted for the next cycle.
4. Repeated the cycle of planning → implementing → reviewing → reflecting.
